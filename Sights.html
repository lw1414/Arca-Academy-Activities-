I · Calculated

The lean is the design.

The tilt is no accident. Five degrees of soft Pisan clay decided the foundation; eight stories of compensating masonry decided everything after. The blueprint reads not as error, but as engineering response.

II · Engineered

The correction.

Each tier built taller on the sinking side, curving the shaft like a banana to chase vertical. Marble columns thread the arcades — beautiful by intent, structural by necessity. Nothing here is decorative.

III · Finished

The marvel.

199 years to build, 800 to stand. The bell tower of Pisa is a controlled fall in marble — proof that the most famous mistake in architecture was, all along, a marvel of structural engineering.

Tests are not the chore.

They are the contract — between the engineer who wrote it and the engineer who will read it at 2 a.m. when something breaks.

What we believe

Software quality is not a department. It is not a checkbox at the end of a Jira ticket. It is the substance of the thing itself — and when a team treats QA as an afterthought, the product becomes one too.

We started Tester.io because the tools we'd been handed treated tests as a cost to minimize. Run them less. Cache them harder. Skip the ones that flake. The result was predictable: a slow erosion of trust, until nobody believed the green checkmark anymore.

Our principles

  1. Speed is a feature, not a tradeoff. A test suite that takes 40 minutes will be disabled, then deleted. We optimize relentlessly for fast feedback.
  2. Flake is a bug. A flaky test isn't telling you the network is weird — it's telling you something about your code, your test, or your environment is wrong. We help you find which.
  3. Honest signals. No vanity dashboards. No "97% pass rate" when 6 of the 200 failures are critical. We surface what matters, in the place it matters.
  4. Author once, run everywhere. Browsers shouldn't fragment your test suite. Neither should mobile.
  5. Open by default. Your test data is yours. Your spec files are portable. There is an export button on every page.

How we work

We're 38 people in eight countries. We ship continuously and dogfood our own platform — every PR to Tester.io is gated by ~14,000 Tester.io tests. We document loudly and write changelogs like we'd want to read them.

If any of this resonates, we'd love to hear from you.
